About The Conference

GULF ENTREPRENEURS FORUM 2024

Under the patronage of His Highness Sheikh Hamad bin Saif Al-Rashdi, Deputy Ruler of Al-Fujairah

Our vision

We strive for the GCC Entrepreneurs Forum to be a leading platform that enhances innovation and collaboration among entrepreneurs, investors, and institutions in the GCC countries, contributing to the building of a sustainable and prosperous investment environment that reflects the aspirations of ambitious GCC youth.

Our mission

We aim through the GCC Entrepreneurs Forum to provide unique opportunities for communication and knowledge exchange between all partners in the entrepreneurship sector. We also work on supporting small and medium-sized companies and enhancing their growth by providing innovative strategies and solutions that align with the current challenges and opportunities in the market.

Our Goals

Building and enhancing partnerships:
 Hear from Building and enhancing strategic relationships between small and medium-sized companies and large institutions, to exchange ideas and experiences.

Facing challenges:
 Assessing the challenges faced by entrepreneurs in the region and developing effective strategies and solutions to support them in overcoming them.

Exploring investment opportunities:
 Discussing and analyzing the available opportunities for investment in various sectors, and guiding entrepreneurs towards suitable options to expand their businesses.

Promoting innovation:
 Providing a conducive environment for exchanging new and innovative ideas and encouraging the development of technological solutions that contribute to improving performance and quality.

Through this vision, message, and objectives, we aim to support the growth and prosperity of the entrepreneurship sector in the region, and enhance the GCC’s position as a global hub for innovation and investment.

Benefits of participating in the forum

Brand enhancement:
 Participating in the forum as a sponsor, supporter, or participant provides an opportunity to increase awareness of the brand and enhance its reputation through presence on a prominent platform and attracting the attention of a diverse audience of business leaders and investors.

Access to a wide network:
 Sponsors, supporters, and participants can access a wide network of top professionals, business leaders, and investors from across the Gulf Cooperation Council countries, opening opportunities for collaboration and building strategic partnerships.

Direct marketing opportunities:
 Through effective participation in various forum events, sponsors can directly market their products or services to interested individuals, contributing to positive sales results.

Promoting social responsibility:
Supporting the forum demonstrates companies' commitment to contributing to economic and social development in the region, enhancing their image as socially responsible entities.

Participation in workshops and discussions:
Sponsors can participate in workshops and discussion sessions held during the forum, allowing them to showcase their expertise and share their opinions with attendees.

Conference Partners
Federation of GCC Chambers

The goals of the Federation of GCC Chambers are to achieve economic integration by coordinating efforts among member chambers, representing the Gulf private sector in official forums, enhancing the legislative and business environment for competitiveness, forming strategic international partnerships to attract investment, fostering innovation, and promoting sustainable development for the Gulf economy.

Secretariat General of the Gulf Cooperation Council

The Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) is a political and economic union of Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE, established in 1981 with its headquarters in Riyadh. All member states are monarchies, and discussions have occurred about potential membership for Jordan, Morocco, and Yemen. The GCC has a military arm, the Peninsula Shield Force, and faced proposals for a tighter Gulf Union in response to regional challenges like the Arab Spring.

Fujairah Chamber of Commerce and Industry

In the early 1980s, Fujairah's private sector expanded significantly, leading to the need for an entity to support and represent it. In response, H.H Sheikh Hamad bin Mohammed Al Sharqi issued a decree in 1981 establishing the Fujairah Chamber of Commerce and Industry as an independent institution. Its purpose is to represent, protect, and develop the commercial and industrial sectors in the interests of its members and the wider UAE.
